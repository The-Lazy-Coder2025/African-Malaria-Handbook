Meet the Creator

Isabelle Lawson is a 17-year-old biology student and malaria researcher from Chiredzi, Zimbabwe.
While the site says “we,” that's just polite cover for “me, myself, and a lot of coffee.”

Isabelle is pursuing her undergraduate degree at Milligan University in Tennessee, where she researches the effects of malaria on the brain and is developing this platform as part of her academic and personal mission.

This resource is the result of years of research, classification, and verification with support from qualified professionals. The goal: make accurate, practical data available to those who need it most.

Please don’t repost, replicate, or claim credit for this work. If you want to collaborate or cite the resource, contact Isabelle.

About the Project

The African Malaria Handbook is a digital initiative mapping drug interactions relevant to African clinical contexts—especially those involving anti-malarials and neurological conditions. It exists to fill a gap in accessible, evidence-based interaction data for healthcare workers across the continent.
